# be-BOP Manifesto

https://be-bop.io/bebop-manifesto

## The priority of a person who wants to share content should be creating that content and sharing it.

While we readily accept that a certain pragmatism must be present (regarding preparation, the work to be done, communication, and the economic and fiscal regulation of earnings), we believe on the other hand that the processes currently available to people who want to share content are at best biased, at worst blocking.

A person who wants to share their content is a person who, beyond their creation, must:
- make themselves visible, virtually or physically
- offer or sell the fruits of their creation
- benefit from patronage by collecting support, donations, sponsorships, and other assistance from their first, second, and third circles
- simply declare the fruits of their creative activities to the relevant authorities

Supporters, whether they are close friends, patrons, informed amateurs, devoted fans, enthusiastic strangers, or simply curious people, should be able to:
- access the news and creations of the person sharing their content
- obtain or acquire said creations in the way that suits them best
- support the person sharing their content in alternative ways, beyond a simple purchase, through a donation, encouragement, service, subscription, or otherwise

And while, in recent years, the emergence of solutions like Sum Up or Twint have allowed content sharers to more easily monetize their creations with their audience during physical meetings, others have suffered the woes of the Fintech ecosystem that had nevertheless been designed and marketed around content sharing with communities:
- the specialization of platforms (one for sales; another for donations; another for subscriptions; another for crowdfunding) has on one hand fragmented the visibility (via SEO) of content creators, and on the other hand complicated the support journey for the public who must identify and share their information with multiple centralized entities; which exploit and monetize personal information from the public, and charge their intermediary role through significant margins on actions that should fundamentally be exclusively between the content sharer and their supporting audience.
- platform censorship: whether it's the sudden shutdown of a service depriving hundreds of thousands of content creators of their revenue by unilateral decision of the payment service provider (Mangopay, with UTip); whether it's attempted modifications of Terms of Use of a community site by these same payment service providers (OnlyFans); whether it's abusive demonetization of multimedia content moderated by algorithms that sometimes allow claims on free music excerpts or fair-use violations that can cut off a creator's revenue without notice with presumption of guilt; or whether it's simply service interruption, changes in economic terms of use, platform closure, or content or access prohibition in certain countries.

Self-hosted solutions, meanwhile, can prove so versatile that they could equip large e-commerce groups, integrate PIMs, MDMs, and mimic the operation and organization of giants (via a freemium base and optional paid plugins that quickly become essential, sometimes even for legal compliance), where the needs of content creators are much simpler, and their skills are creative skills, not skills for managing an advanced e-commerce system requiring costly web agency interventions.

Therefore, to address these increasingly present issues among content creators, we decided to design be-BOP:
- by offering a hosted solution without plugins, open-source and free, for greater stability, and to benefit from a complete ecosystem, activatable or not according to needs, without hidden additional fees (merch, CMS, ARM, analytics, billing tracking, i18n, accessibility...)
- by directly including SEO simplicity: no more double entry of product information and static pages versus meta tags, what is displayed on the site is what is indexed by SEO crawlers
- by allowing a supporter, within the same journey, to make a purchase, a donation, a periodic subscription, and a pre-order without needing to juggle between several specialized platforms
- by natively including connection to resilient and uncensorable payment methods (Bitcoin and Lightning), to popular and widespread payment service providers (for online card payments), to more exotic or localized methods for worldwide sharing (iDeal, SoFort, AliPay), to payment methods used by unbanked populations deprived of international access to culture (mobile money and solutions like Machankura), as well as offline payments to enable both online operation and point-of-sale systems in showrooms or stores
- by respecting customer personal information (login via temporary session link or third-party connection via SSO, without storing customer passwords in database to preserve their digital identity in case of merchant hacking; minimal use of customer personal information — no postal address request if no physical consideration during a purchase or in case of a simple donation; express customer consent for IP address retention in case of merchant accounting needs, similar to accepting ToS during the order process...)
- by offering content creators all the tools to manage their legal and accounting obligations according to their regime, their location, and that of the customer (all worldwide VAT rates stored in the code; 4 modular and configurable VAT regimes to meet accounting and customs needs for cross-border purchases; configurable retention of customer information in case of legal obligation...)
- by allowing the public to use selfcare via an innovative, secure, decentralized, and uncensorable communication protocol (Nostr), as an alternative to the eternal, monetized, and often misused email address (which nevertheless remains present and usable)

Through our platform, we want to put sharing back at the center of the creation chain: it is a peer-to-peer action between a content creator and a person who wishes to support and encourage them.
And for this, our levers are digital sovereignty, uncensorability, internationalization, code ethics both for the platform manager and their audience, and going against certain SaaS trends that have now become clichés.

> The world of sharing must replace the sharing of the world - **Claude Lelouch**

> Without culture, and the relative freedom it implies, society, even perfect, is nothing but a jungle. That is why every authentic creation is a gift to the future. - **Albert Camus**

## The Future of be-BOP and Decentralized Marketplaces

> Distinguish well emulation from envy:
> One leads to glory, and the other to dishonor;
> One is the nourishment of genius,
> And the other is the poison of the heart.
> **Voltaire**

In content creation, as in craftsmanship, writing, or many other fields, one might think that you need to be competitive, elbow your way through, and crush others to break through, make your place, and succeed.
This has, for example, led to what we consider certain digital abuses such as SEA (Search Engine Advertising), where the company that paid the most could end up at the top of results for certain keywords in certain search engines, or visibility boosts and similar premium formulas on marketplaces or monetized community sites.

It is certain that, once upon a time, the use of certain centralized platforms like Kickstarter allowed attracting a new audience (the holy grail of the third circle) if the product and communication lent themselves to an audience seeking innovation.
However, this natural audience acquisition has diminished over time, with the growing popularity of platforms, the multiplication of projects, and the audience's habit of seeking the most successful projects to obtain pre-order packs at preferential rates. And so much so that, for certain community monetization platforms, it is almost exclusively the platform's meta-information that is communicated to crawler robots for SEO, and not the content creator's information, their projects, and their funding campaigns, leading users into a sort of dead end limiting them to their first and second circles.
Similarly, large centralized marketplaces like CDiscount or Amazon allowed greater visibility, equated with better distribution. But popularity algorithms, push mechanisms, the multiplicity of sellers (some very large, some generalist, some in countries with more favorable tax regimes), and the commercial conditions of these platforms have sidelined the most modest creators, despite the rare success stories put forward. When it's not simply about service exclusion and invisibilization of certain content, such as that concerning 18+ video game content creators.

On the other hand, collaboration between creators, the "Capsule" effect, featuring, and partnerships have a virtuous and timeless effect: a joint creation, a shared event, a project mixing universes — this type of action tends to bring communities and supporters together. Creators, hand in hand, bring their communities to meet and mingle.
But this kind of thing, from an IT perspective and currently, is only possible through the creation of dedicated platforms, or ill-suited centralized services.

With the be-BOP product, we can currently offer content creators a self-hosted solution to share their content and monetize their community.

With the Cross-be-BOP function, we will be able, through a Nostr-based association mechanism, to allow two or more be-BOPs to:
- authorize direct collaboration
- perform automatic catalog exchanges to sell each other's products
- automatically apply margins and commissions previously configured in each be-BOP's back-office, with automatic sending via Lightning
- send billing orders to the original creator's be-BOP to automatically track the affiliated sale in their accounting and stock management
- conduct participatory funding campaigns (called peerfunding because without third-party fund retention) combining everyone's creations

Finally, with the Meta-be-BOP function, we want to reinvent the role of the influencer and the classic use of promo codes or affiliate links to enable the creation of showrooms for selections and favorites.
Under the activation of a Discovery parameter by the content creator owning a be-BOP, with the definition of a margin percentage (Discovery commission), anyone could open their own be-BOP and populate it from these public catalogs, similar to Cross-be-BOP:
- A literary influencer could showcase their selection of books from different publishing houses, accompanied by their reviews, and receive the sales commission defined by those publishers
- An NGO could present a selection of humanitarian or charitable peerfunding projects and campaigns by simply making them visible, without claiming a margin
- A be-BOP owner equipped with a Bitcoin node, a Lightning relay, and several fiat payment methods (such as card, SoFort, AliPay, wire transfer, or other) could allow be-BOP owners who don't have access to these services to reach a wider audience that wouldn't be limited to their sole accessible payment method

And all of this in a fully decentralized manner: no central API, no marketplace server acting as an information node, with star-pattern communication between each store through Nostr bots linked to each be-BOP's back-end, configured by the content creator, to enable resilient, decentralized, and peer-to-peer sharing of their content to a wider audience.

Through these Cross-be-BOP and Meta-be-BOP functions, we hope to encourage ephemeral operations, events, festivals, artistic cooperatives, where content creators could work together but autonomously to go beyond the scope of their sole activity, and benefit from the selection of strangers who would value the quality of their creations beyond a simple review, through genuine artistic or artisanal curation.
